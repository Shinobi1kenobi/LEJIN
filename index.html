1. Introduction and Scope

This report compiles open-source data, classified and unclassified references, geological surveys, and military standards to analyze the Diablo Range region, focusing on Mount Oso and associated infrastructures. It explores the strategic utility of the area for classified Department of Defense (DoD) and Department of Energy (DOE) operations, emphasizing subterranean, AI, and thermal management projects likely linked to national security.

2. Geographic Overview and Coordinates

  • Mount Oso: 37.6297° N, 121.2741° W; Elevation: 3,352 ft (1,021 m)
  • Ingram Canyon: Southwest of Mount Oso; similar basalt geology
  • California Aqueduct and Dos Amigos Pumping Plant: ~36.9238° N, 120.8288° W
  • Mount Diablo: Central Diablo Range peak with naval communication installation

Private gated land with no formal public records or street addresses.

14. Conclusions and Future Considerations

The Diablo Range, including Mount Oso and Ingram Canyon, represents a highly secure and strategically optimized region for clandestine military, AI, quantum, and national security operations.
Natural geological features such as basalt and proximity to the California Aqueduct provide ideal conditions for subterranean installations that require EM shielding and thermal regulation.
Communication infrastructure is discreet and sophisticated, ensuring secure data transmission consistent with DoD and DOE classified operations.
Military-standard roads and strict airspace and land access controls strongly suggest ongoing, active use by military or federal agencies.
Environmental indicators like vegetation stress and wildlife avoidance could serve as indirect evidence of underground activity.
This report recommends ongoing monitoring using remote sensing, thermal imaging, and geophysical surveys to detect potential new developments or changes in activity.
